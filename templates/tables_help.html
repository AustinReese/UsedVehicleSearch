About quantile tables

Quantile tables are a somewhat complicated tool to read. The intent is to allow users to gauge what is considered to be a 'good price' or 'bad price' for a vehicle by filtering vehicle options and displaying the average price (or year/odometer) in the Nth percentile of the result. These average prices are grouped by another numerical variable for further insight into the current going rate of used vehicles. Take the following table as an example. The display field is price, grouped by year with no filters applied.

Say you wanted to purchase a vehicle from 2009. This falls in the 2008-2010 year group, and we see that the best price (1-10th percentile, unlike many applications of percentiles a low 'score' is actually a good thing) is somewhere around $2,607, with $4,397 and $5,383 falling in the 11-20th and 21-30th percentiles respectively. Poor prices are above $11,760, 71-80th percentile, with the most expensive of all used cars from 2008-2010 costing an average of $29,065. These price differences are high as this table was not filtered on any vehicle criteria, but using the vehicle filter in along with the quantile tool allows one to build highly specific tables which are good indicators as to the current market value of the vehicle. Due to the size of the database and computational cost of building the tables, the number of listings used to build tables is capped at 10k. That being said, specific searches are likely to return less than 10k listings.
